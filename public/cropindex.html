Wheat (Triticum)

General Information

Wheat is one of the major cereal crops globally grown for its seeds, which are ground to make flour for various food products like bread.

Water Usage

Wheat requires around 450-650 mm of water throughout its growth cycle.

Diseases

Common diseases include rust, smut, and mildew, which can cause damage to the crop.

Solution for Diseases

Proper crop rotation, resistant varieties, and fungicide application can help control wheat diseases.

Rain Requirements

Wheat requires moderate rainfall, typically during the germination phase. Excessive rain during harvest can harm the crop.

Rice (Oryza sativa)

General Information

Rice is a staple food crop for more than half of the world's population, especially in Asia.

Water Usage

Rice is grown in flooded fields and requires approximately 2500 liters of water to produce 1 kg of rice.

Diseases

Rice is susceptible to diseases such as blast, brown spot, and bacterial leaf blight.

Solution for Diseases

Using resistant varieties and applying fungicides can control rice diseases effectively.

Rain Requirements

Rice needs abundant rainfall during the growing period and is generally grown in regions with high rainfall.

Millet (Pennisetum glaucum)

General Information

Millet is a drought-tolerant grain that grows well in arid and semi-arid regions. It is a staple food in parts of Africa and Asia.

Water Usage

Millet requires less water compared to other cereal crops, needing around 300-400 mm of water during its growth period.

Diseases

Common diseases include downy mildew and smut, which can affect crop yields.

Solution for Diseases

Crop rotation and disease-resistant varieties can help prevent millet diseases.

Rain Requirements

Millet thrives in regions with low rainfall and can withstand dry conditions.
