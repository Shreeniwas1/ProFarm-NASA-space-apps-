Clay Soil

Clay soil is composed of very fine particles and retains water well, making it suitable for certain types of crops like rice and wheat.

Learn More

Sandy Soil

Sandy soil has larger particles, allowing for good drainage, but it doesn't retain nutrients well. It's ideal for root vegetables like carrots.

Learn More

Silt Soil

Silt soil is fertile and has good water retention capabilities, often found in river valleys, making it great for growing crops like rice.

Learn More

Loam Soil

Loam soil is a combination of sand, silt, and clay. It's considered the perfect soil for growing most types of plants due to its balance of properties.
