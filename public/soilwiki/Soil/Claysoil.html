Clay Soil

Information about Clay Soil

Clay soil is heavy and can be easily compacted. It retains moisture and is rich in nutrients but can have drainage problems. It is ideal for certain crops when properly managed.

Soil Type (Acidic/basic/neutral)

Clay soil tends to be neutral to slightly alkaline, but its pH can vary depending on the region. Testing is recommended to determine the exact pH level.

Suited for these Crops

Examples of crops suited for clay soil include Broccoli, Cabbage, and Peas. Many root crops, such as Potatoes, also do well in clay soil.

Regions of Occurrence

Clay soils are commonly found in regions with slow-draining areas, particularly in places with heavy rainfall or where there are natural clay deposits.
