Loam Soil

Information about Loam Soil

Loam soil is a balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay, offering excellent drainage and nutrient retention. It's the most fertile soil type, making it ideal for farming.

Soil Type (Acidic/basic/neutral)

Loam soil tends to have a neutral pH, making it suitable for a wide variety of plants. However, soil testing is recommended to determine the exact pH and nutrient content.

Suited for these Crops

Loam soil supports a wide range of crops, including Wheat, Sugarcane, Cotton, and Pulses. Its versatility makes it one of the best soils for farming.

Regions of Occurrence

Loam soils are commonly found in regions with balanced rainfall. They are widespread in areas where agriculture is predominant.
